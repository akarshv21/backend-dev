Q1. Stock Trading Platform
Answer: CP (Consistency + Partition Tolerance)
. Requires strong consistency → correct stock prices and transactions
. Cannot allow stale or incorrect data
. System must handle network failures (partition tolerance)
. Availability can be sacrificed temporarily
Justification: Correct data is critical in trading, so consistency is prioritized over availability.

Q2. Content Delivery Network (CDN)
Answer: AP (Availability + Partition Tolerance)
. Needs high availability → content must always be accessible
. Can tolerate slightly outdated data (eventual consistency)
. Works across distributed global servers
. Handles network partitions effectively
Justification: Fast and always-available content is more important than perfect consistency.

Q3. Airline Booking System
Answer: CP (Consistency + Partition Tolerance)
. Requires accurate seat booking (no double booking)
. Strong consistency is essential
. Must handle distributed systems (partition tolerance)
. Availability may be reduced during failures
Justification: Avoiding booking conflicts is critical, so consistency is prioritized.

Q4. Video Streaming Service
Answer: AP (Availability + Partition Tolerance)
. Requires high availability → users should always access videos
. Can tolerate minor delays or outdated data
. Distributed across multiple servers globally
. Ensures smooth streaming even during failures
Justification: Continuous service is more important than strict consistency.
